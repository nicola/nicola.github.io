Nicola Greco
{% marginfigure ../assets/img/nicola.jpg 'Photo by [Virginia Alonso](http://virginialonso.com)' %}
is a computer scientist with focus on decentralized systems, applied cryptography and multi-agent security. He directed protocol design &amp; applied cryptography research and run large scale research funding programs at several organizations including *ARIA*, *Protocol Labs*, *MIT*. He run an investment vehicle called *deepcrypto*.

He started writing code when he was very young. At __14__ run a petition _“Linux in Italian Schools”_, converting schools to use Open Source Software; __16__, started a _BuddyPress’ developers community_, an open source platform for federated social networks; __18__, wrote software for Social Network analysis used by Telecom Italia, which featured at *TEDx* and on *Wired Italian Top 10 under 25*; __21__, left his undergraduate program at UCL a year before graduation to join MIT as a PhD student to work on decentralized web technologies with Sir Tim Berners-Lee and to join the Harvard Berkman-Klein Center for Internet and Society as a fellow; __23__, joined Protocol Labs to run the applied cryptography research team for 7+ years, participating in the design of several decentralized protocols widely used today; __27__, started [deepcrypto](https://deepcrypto.xyz), an investment vehicle for early stage cryptography companies; __30__, he started several organizations including [Filoz](https://filoz.org), [Public Good Crypto](https://publicgoodcrypto.org) and [Quantum Punks](https://quantumpunks.org) a new movement to make quantum technology practical; __32__, he co-designed a £50M research funding program at [ARIA](https://scalingtrust.org.uk) with Alex Obadia.

About me

Nicola Greco
Photo by Virginia Alonso is a computer scientist with focus on decentralized systems, applied cryptography and multi-agent security. He directed protocol design & applied cryptography research and run large scale research funding programs at several organizations including ARIA, Protocol Labs, MIT. He run an investment vehicle called deepcrypto.

He started writing code when he was very young. At 14 run a petition “Linux in Italian Schools”, converting schools to use Open Source Software; 16, started a BuddyPress’ developers community, an open source platform for federated social networks; 18, wrote software for Social Network analysis used by Telecom Italia, which featured at TEDx and on Wired Italian Top 10 under 25; 21, left his undergraduate program at UCL a year before graduation to join MIT as a PhD student to work on decentralized web technologies with Sir Tim Berners-Lee and to join the Harvard Berkman-Klein Center for Internet and Society as a fellow; 23, joined Protocol Labs to run the applied cryptography research team for 7+ years, participating in the design of several decentralized protocols widely used today; 27, started deepcrypto, an investment vehicle for early stage cryptography companies; 30, he started several organizations including Filoz, Public Good Crypto and Quantum Punks a new movement to make quantum technology practical; 32, he co-designed a £50M research funding program at ARIA with Alex Obadia.
